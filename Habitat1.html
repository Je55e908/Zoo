This is the desert habitat

The desert habitat is a unique and challenging ecosystem characterized by arid and dry conditions, where water is scarce and temperatures can be extreme. Despite these harsh conditions, a variety of specialized plants and animals have adapted to thrive in deserts around the world. Desert plants, such as cacti and succulents, have evolved to store water and reduce water loss, while desert animals like camels and kangaroo rats have developed remarkable adaptations for water conservation.

FENNEC FOX

Fennec foxes are the smallest species of fox, with a length of about 9-16 inches (excluding their 7-12 inch tail) and a weight of 1.5-3.5 pounds. They have long, pointed ears that can measure up to 6 inches, which help them dissipate heat and locate prey. Their fur is typically pale yellow to sandy beige color, which provides camouflage in their desert habitat. These foxes are omnivorous, feeding on a diet that includes small mammals, insects, birds, eggs, and desert vegetation. They have keen senses of hearing and smell, which helps them locate prey underground.

camels

Camels are large, iconic mammals known for their adaptability to harsh desert environments. Camels are well-suited to desert life with their long legs, broad, tough feet that allow them to walk on hot sand, and a hump (or two) that stores fat, not water as commonly believed. The hump(s) serve as energy reserves, allowing camels to survive without water for extended periods.
