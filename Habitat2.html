Forest Habitat!!!!!!

A forest habitat is a diverse and complex ecosystem characterized by a dense growth of trees, shrubs, and other vegetation. Forests cover a significant portion of the Earth's surface and can be found in various forms across different climates and regions. These habitats are essential for both wildlife and humans, providing a wide range of ecological, economic, and recreational benefits.

Deer

Deer are graceful and herbivorous mammals belonging to the family Cervidae. They are known for their distinctive antlers, which are typically found on males (bucks)Deer vary in size and coloration, but they generally have slender bodies with long legs and a tail. Their fur can range from reddish-brown to grayish-brown, depending on the species and location.

Coyote

The coyote, scientifically known as Canis latrans, is a highly adaptable and intelligent carnivorous mammal native to North and Central America. These canids are known for their distinctive howling calls and play a crucial role in various ecosystems as both predator and scavenger. Coyotes have a varied diet, including small mammals, birds, fruits, and carrion. They are opportunistic and have successfully adapted to urban environments. Coyotes are known for their cunning behavior and are often depicted in folklore and myths in Native American cultures. They are medium-sized animals with a grayish-brown coat, bushy tail, and a keen sense of survival.
